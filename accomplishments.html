Swami Vivekanada
  • 1863-1902

Early Life

Swami Vivekananda, born as Narendranath Datta, entered the world on January 12, 1863, in Calcutta, British India. From a young age, he displayed remarkable intelligence and an inquisitive mind. Narendranath's early years were influenced by his parents, especially his father, Vishwanath Datta, who was an attorney with a rational and liberal outlook. Narendranath's upbringing was marked by exposure to a diverse range of philosophical and religious ideas, fostering an environment that encouraged intellectual exploration.

Page 2

During his childhood and teenage years, Narendranath developed a keen interest in spirituality and the quest for truth. His inquisitive nature led him to question the traditional beliefs and practices of his time. Despite facing personal challenges, including the early loss of his father, Narendranath's intellectual pursuits continued to blossom. As a student, he excelled in academics and became known for his exceptional memory and oratory skills. His early encounters with the influential spiritual leader Ramakrishna Paramahamsa would later profoundly shape the course of his life.

Page 3

The pivotal turning point in Narendranath's early life occurred when he met Ramakrishna, who recognized the young man's spiritual potential. Narendranath became a devoted disciple of Ramakrishna, and this relationship laid the foundation for his spiritual journey. Under Ramakrishna's guidance, he delved deep into the study of various religious traditions and experienced profound spiritual realizations. This transformative period set the stage for the emergence of Swami Vivekananda, who would go on to become a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies to the Western world.
